Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions we hear from prospective clients.

How much does a personal injury lawyer cost in Dallas?
At Sterling & Associates, we work on a contingency fee basis — meaning you pay nothing unless we win your case. Our fee is a percentage of your settlement or verdict, so there is zero upfront cost or financial risk to you. If we don't recover money for you, you owe us nothing.
What is the statute of limitations for personal injury in Texas?
In Texas, you generally have two years from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit. Missing this deadline can permanently bar your right to compensation — even if your claim is otherwise valid. This is why it's critical to consult an attorney as soon as possible after an accident.
How long does a personal injury case take to settle?
The timeline varies based on injury severity, liability disputes, and insurance company cooperation. Minor cases may resolve in 3–6 months; complex cases involving serious injuries can take 1–3 years. We always pursue the fastest resolution that still maximizes your compensation — we never rush a settlement just to close a file.
What should I do immediately after a car accident in Texas?
Call 911 immediately. Seek medical attention even if you feel fine — some injuries appear days later. Document the scene with photos. Exchange information with all parties involved. Get witness contact information. Most importantly: call Sterling & Associates before speaking with any insurance adjuster. Insurance companies record those calls and use them against you.
Do I have a case if I was partially at fault for the accident?
Yes — Texas follows a modified comparative fault rule. You can recover damages as long as you were less than 51% at fault for the accident. Your compensation is reduced by your percentage of fault. For example: if you were 20% at fault and your damages total $100,000, you can still recover $80,000. Don't assume you have no case — call us for a free review.
