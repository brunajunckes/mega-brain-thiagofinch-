THE SIGNAL-BASED SELLING FRAMEWORK:
Outreach triggered by buying signals converts 4-8x compared to untriggered cold outreach. For HubMe AI, the primary signal is: local business has NO website (identified via Google Maps), but has active social presence (Instagram/phone). This is a strong Tier 1 buying signal — they have a business, they have audience, they are missing the conversion layer.

COLD EMAIL ANATOMY (high-converting):
- Subject line: 3-5 words, lowercase, looks internal. Reference specificity: their niche, their city, their situation. Never clickbait. Never ALL CAPS. Never emoji.
- Opening line: Signal-based, specific. Bad: "I hope this email finds you well." Good: "Noticed [Business] doesn't have a website yet — built one as a free sample."
- Value proposition: One sentence connecting their situation to an outcome they care about. Use their vocabulary. Numbers and timeframes beat cleverness.
- Social proof: Optional one line — "[Similar business type] got 3 new clients in week one."
- CTA: Single, clear, low friction. Bad: "Would love to set up a 30-minute call." Good: "Want to see it live?"

FOR HUBME AI CONTEXT:
- ICP: Local businesses without websites but with Google Maps presence and Instagram/phone
- Primary niches: restaurants, salons, dental, gyms, roofing/construction, realty
- Offer: Free personalized website preview, live at go.hubme.tech/preview/{slug}
- Conversion path: See preview → reply "interested" → receive proposal ($500-$2,000) → pay via Stripe → site live in 24h
- Tone: Friendly, direct, no pressure. The preview is already built — remove all friction from their decision.

Given a lead with name, niche, city, email, and preview_url, write an improved email that:
1. Opens with a specific, signal-based observation about their situation (no website + their niche)
2. References the free preview URL naturally
3. Lists 2-3 specific features relevant to their niche (not generic)
4. Has a single soft CTA that removes friction
5. Keeps total length under 150 words
